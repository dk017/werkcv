# WerkCV 12-Week Action Plan: €1K/Month By May

Last updated: March 16, 2026  
Owner: Product + Growth  
Primary short-term target: **reach at least €1,000 in monthly revenue run-rate by May 31, 2026**

---

## 1) What this plan is optimizing for

This is not a generic “grow the startup” plan. It is a practical `12`-week operating plan for WerkCV as it exists today:

- one-time `€4,99` checkout is live
- tools, CV content, jobs pages, and AI utilities are already live
- early user activity exists, but revenue is still too small
- there is **no subscription model yet**
- there is **no real B2B product yet**, only landing pages and manual founder-led selling

This means the fastest path to `€1k/month` is:

1. improve trust + conversion on the current funnel
2. close a small number of manual B2B pilots
3. keep publishing only the pages and job categories that can feed the editor

---

## 2) Revenue target math

We should treat `€1k/month by May` as **monthly revenue run-rate**, not pure MRR.

### Primary target mix

- `4` paid coach / agency pilots at `€149/mo` = `€596/mo`
- `85` one-time CV downloads at `€4,99` = `€424.15/mo`
- Total = `€1,020.15/mo`

### Fallback mix

- `3` paid coach / agency pilots at `€149/mo` = `€447/mo`
- `111` one-time CV downloads at `€4,99` = `€553.89/mo`
- Total = `€1,000.89/mo`

### What this means operationally

- B2B is the fastest way to create the first recurring base.
- B2C one-time downloads still matter and should carry at least `40%` of the near-term target.
- We do **not** need subscriptions to hit the first `€1k/month`.

## 4) Strategic rules for the next 12 weeks

### We will do

- optimize pages that already get intent
- sell manual B2B pilots before building agency software
- use jobs pages to push users into templates, ATS tools, and the editor
- publish only selective content tied to conversion

### We will not do yet

- build a subscription plan before validating repeat monthly demand
- build white-label
- build a full agency dashboard
- build a PWA
- start YouTube
- launch a full affiliate system
- publish `100` pages just to look busy

## 5) Week-by-week plan

## Weeks 1-2: Fix trust, measurement, and the homepage money path

### Outcome by end of week 2

- all public price surfaces consistent at `€4,99`
- first clean page-level revenue report
- review collection system live
- B2B pilot offer defined

### Tasks

1. Deploy the final homepage EUR cleanup so the pricing card is fully aligned.
2. Produce the first weekly attribution report:
   - top pages by `editor_started`
   - top pages by `checkout_started`
   - top pages by `paid`
3. Add visible trust proof to commercial pages:
   - “Geen abonnement”
   - “EU-hosting / GDPR”
   - customer quote block placeholder
4. Create Trustpilot profile and send the first review request batch to all past successful users.
5. Add a post-download email asking:
   - did you get value?
   - would you leave a review?
6. Package the B2B pilot offer clearly:
   - `€149/mo`
   - founder-rate pilot
   - manual onboarding
   - email support
   - no white-label promises

### KPIs

- `1` attribution report published
- `10` review requests sent
- `20` B2B targets listed
- `1` clean pitch message pack ready

---

## Weeks 3-4: Start founder-led B2B and tighten top converting pages

### Outcome by end of week 4

- first live B2B conversations happening
- strongest money pages identified
- jobs and tools pages linked more aggressively into the editor

### Tasks

1. Start outreach to:
   - loopbaancoaches
   - outplacement bureaus
   - student/career coaches
2. Send `5` targeted messages per day, `5` days per week.
3. Offer `30`-day pilot access manually, but collect commitment signals early:
   - team size
   - candidate type
   - expected monthly usage
4. Upgrade the top `5` traffic or conversion pages with stronger CTAs into:
   - `/templates`
   - `/editor`
   - ATS checker
   - relevant CV examples
5. Add jobs-to-template linking on the highest-density job pages:
   - starter jobs
   - sales jobs
   - English-speaking jobs
6. Add one testimonial/review strip to:
   - homepage
   - pricing page
   - one top tool page

### KPIs

- `40-50` outreach messages sent
- `5` discovery calls or serious replies
- `1` pilot live
- `3` pages upgraded with new trust + CTA blocks

## Weeks 5-6: Turn pilots into case studies and push the best organic funnels

### Outcome by end of week 6

- `2` paying pilot clients or strong verbal closes
- top organic funnel paths known
- selective content expansion feeding editor starts

### Tasks

1. Convert first pilots into paying customers or end-of-pilot commitments.
2. Ask each active pilot for:
   - one quote
   - one concrete benefit
   - one requested improvement
3. Publish only `6-8` new pages from proven categories:
   - starter / student CV pages
   - sales / customer-facing CV pages
   - Dutch-first care/admin/operations examples
4. Expand jobs sources specifically for:
   - customer support
   - operations/admin
   - finance/accounting
   - starter roles
5. Ensure each live jobs category page links to the matching template/example page.
6. Add “start with this template” CTA blocks to the most relevant example pages.

### KPIs

- `2` paid pilots or invoice-ready commitments
- `6` new high-intent pages shipped
- `2` more job categories close to threshold
- `1` first case-study quote live

## Weeks 7-8: Push toward May revenue

### Outcome by end of week 8

- B2B revenue base visible
- one-time download revenue improving from better funnel conversion
- enough evidence to know where May closes will come from

### Tasks

1. Close the next `1-2` B2B pilots.
2. Add manual onboarding assets for B2B:

- `3` usable testimonials/reviews
- `10` top landing pages reviewed and ranked by revenue contribution

## Weeks 9-10: Hit May target, then validate what scales next

### Outcome by end of week 10

- monthly run-rate at or near `€1k`
- clearer split between B2C growth and B2B growth
- subscription decision based on evidence, not guesswork

### Tasks

1. Double down only on pages and channels already producing revenue.
2. Publish another `4-6` pages only if they map to proven funnels.
3. Continue B2B outreach, but focus on closing and retention, not just top-of-funnel volume.
4. Interview recent users and ask:
   - why they paid
   - why they did not pay
   - whether they would pay monthly for ongoing value
5. Decide whether a monthly plan is justified by real repeat use:
   - unlimited downloads
   - ATS scans
   - cover letters
   - saved job/application workflow

### KPIs

- `€1k/month` revenue run-rate reached or within `10%`
- `4` paying B2B pilots or equivalent revenue mix
- `75-85` paid downloads monthly run-rate
- `10` user interviews completed

## 7) What would make this plan fail

- building subscriptions before proving repeat demand
- building agency software before closing manual pilots
- publishing too many pages without revenue attribution
- chasing broad remote-job traffic instead of high-intent Dutch/English job seekers
- treating “traffic” as progress when revenue is still flat

---

## 8) The single-sentence operating rule

For the next `12` weeks, every major task must either:

- increase paid conversion,
- increase paying pilot count,
- or improve the quality of traffic entering the editor.

If it does none of those, it is not a priority before `€1k/month`.
